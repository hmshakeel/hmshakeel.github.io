Research profile

Interpretable AI, clinical prediction modelling and health informatics for decision support that can be understood, validated and used by practitioners.

Research Identity

Bridging machine-learning methods and clinical meaning.

Dr Hafiz Muhammad Shakeel works at the intersection of machine learning, explainable AI, mental-health informatics and applied data science. His current research focuses on interpretable prediction modelling for neurodevelopmental research, including ADHD medication-response analysis and multimodal clinical data.

The central research question is practical as well as methodological: how can predictive models be made robust enough for research use, transparent enough for clinical interpretation and useful enough for decision-support workflows?

Research theme

Responsible AI needs transparent modelling, careful validation and outputs that support expert judgement.

Core Programmes

Four research strands with a shared emphasis on interpretability.

Each strand connects model development with the realities of clinical data, operational constraints and stakeholder-facing explanation.

Prediction modelling Feature engineering, validation strategy, model comparison and reproducible ML pipelines.
Explainable AI SHAP, LIME, patient-level interpretation, transparent workflows and responsible reporting.
Health informatics ADHD research, mental-health informatics, clinical workflow and neurodevelopmental data.
Decision support Visual analytics, stakeholder-facing outputs and applied service-improvement systems.
